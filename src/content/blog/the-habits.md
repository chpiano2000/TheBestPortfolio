# The nervous system principle

We have biological system, ad it's filled with all kinds of nerves, wires and little cells flying around. Thus, what we need to is **condition ourselves physically**, not just mentally, but the **actual body**.

How? We do that by *creating patterns, rhythms, routines* that we install into our daily habits.

A good example is **Timing**: go to bed at the same time every night, getting up at the same time, every morning, ...

So, by doing whatever habit roughly at the same time everyday, you're creating a *rhythm*, teaching your nervous system to essentially create crave having that activity happen at the time.

# All in principle

Don't make a habit half effort. Don't make it leap into your habit, but really go for it.

# Speed of implementation principle

When you get an idea, go for it, take it right away, absolutely start to implement immediately. This could be some action like: writing down the idea, or doing some research online, or go buy something online.

The idea is to use the power of the initial action to keep going and take the next action because the **speed of implementation** is a huge part of creating powerful habits.

Next, **no exception**. This means once you start a habits, don't allow yourself to have an exception.

# No people are stronger than habits

We are the creation of what we regularly go or what we don't do. We are not stronger than our habits, so we need our habits to make us strong by following through with everything.

Implementing a habits right away, having no exceptions will **teach our nervous system actually follow through** with what we need to do, and will gives us the **ability to follow through**

# Summary

These principles trains the nervous system to obey, so that you can obey it because no people are stronger than habits.
